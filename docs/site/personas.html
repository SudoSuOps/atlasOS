Personas & ENS

The subdomain comes with the box.

When you buy a HACKER, you don't just get hardware. You get a seat on the network. Your subdomain at <your-name>.atlasos.eth is the identity, the endpoint, and the confidentiality boundary all at once.

The four functions of your subdomain

FunctionWhat it does
Identity Other HACKERs resolve <your-name>.atlasos.eth to your wallet. When you ship an LOI, the network verifies the signature against this. No impersonation possible.
Endpoint discovery Your subdomain also resolves to your live HACKER endpoint (or the gateway proxying it). When Rainmaker matches inventory to your timeline, it routes to your box, not a cloud mailbox.
Confidentiality boundary Every NDA, OM, AIOV, and LOI is encrypted to your wallet's public key. Your subdomain unlocks them. Without your wallet, no one — including us — can decrypt.
Cross-firm portability A receipt at om.defendable.eth/<hash> carries your subdomain in its parent_anchors. Counsel at any firm verifies the chain in 30 seconds. No platform onboarding.

Why ENS is the sovereignty primitive

Every other identity model is platform-locked.

  • A Crexi user ID belongs to Crexi. They can revoke it, sell it, log it.
  • A CoStar login belongs to CoStar.
  • An OAuth token belongs to whoever issued it.
  • An API key belongs to the provider.

If your identity lives on someone else's database, every transaction routes through that someone. They see the deal. They keep a copy. They charge rent. They become the marketplace.

ENS flips it. <your-name>.atlasos.eth resolves to a wallet your HACKER controls via a seed phrase you hold. The seed phrase is not on Swarm and Bee LLC servers — cannot be on our servers. The identity is yours by ECDSA mathematics, not by terms of service.

This is what makes the privacy claim survive an audit

When we say "we can't see your deal even if we wanted to," it's true because the encrypted return travels to your subdomain's wallet · we never hold the decryption key · we cannot be subpoenaed for what we don't have.

What happens if you sell your box

The subdomain stays with the box (or transfers to the new owner's wallet at their choice). HACKER provisioning records are anchored at provisioning.atlasos.eth, so chain of custody is auditable.

If the box is lost or destroyed, the subdomain can be re-bound to a new wallet only if you have retained the seed phrase. This is your responsibility — sovereign data also means sovereign key management. We can't reset what we don't hold.

What happens if you stop being a customer

Nothing. Your box keeps working. Your subdomain stays bound. Past receipts at <type>.defendable.eth/<hash> remain verifiable forever — they're on Hedera.

Subscription products (AIOV subscription, Rainmaker matching) lapse on non-renewal, but the box itself is yours, the OS is yours, and the cooked weights you have are yours. We don't brick boxes.

The customer relationship is term-bounded; your property rights aren't. That's the non-extractive part of the model.

The two-tree ENS architecture

AtlasOS uses ENS in two complementary trees:

TreeRoleResolves to
atlasos.ethWHO · identity · persona · agent · live HACKER endpointwallet address + endpoint URL
defendable.ethWHAT · certified document · receipt · audit chainhash + parent chain on Hedera

They compose. harvey.atlasos.eth ships an underwrite, the receipt lands at deed.defendable.eth/<hash> with parent_anchors pointing back to harvey's wallet. The actor and the artifact are both addressable, both verifiable, both portable across firms.
