Getting started

Power on. Register. Fund. Order.

From cardboard box to your first AIOV in about fifteen minutes. Same flow whether you bought a HACKER ($250), HACKER-PRO ($599), or HACKER-AGX ($2,000).

Before you begin

You'll need: your HACKER, an ethernet cable (preferred · WiFi works as fallback), an HDMI display for first boot, a USB keyboard, and a way to fund a wallet (USDC on Base or Optimism). If you don't have USDC yet, see step 3 — your HACKER ships with a $50 starter balance for the first AIOV on us.

Step 1 · Power on

Plug your HACKER into ethernet, HDMI, USB keyboard, and the included USB-C power supply. The box boots in about 12 seconds and lands on the AtlasOS first-run screen.

The first-run screen shows three things:

  • Box serial. Memorize it or photograph it · this is the device-level identity bound to your subdomain.
  • Network status. Ethernet primary · WiFi fallback · Hedera mirror node connection · ENS resolver connection.
  • Provisioning code. A 6-word phrase you'll enter on a separate device to complete subdomain registration.

Step 2 · Register your subdomain

From your laptop or phone (any device with a browser), navigate to:

https://provision.atlasos.eth/<your-provisioning-code>

You'll be asked for:

  • Subdomain choice. What you want to be called on the network. harvey.atlasos.eth for a brokerage Hack named Harvey · familyofficexyz.atlasos.eth for a family office · kerco.atlasos.eth for a sponsor named Kerco. Subject to availability.
  • Wallet generation OR import. The HACKER can generate a fresh wallet for you (recommended for new users · we never see the seed phrase) or import an existing wallet via seed phrase.
  • Backup confirmation. If you generated a fresh wallet, write down the 12-word seed phrase on the included recovery card. This is the only copy. We can't restore it. Store the card in your safe, your safe deposit box, or your password manager — your call. Sovereign data also means sovereign key management.
Don't lose the seed phrase

The seed phrase is the only key to your wallet, your subdomain, and every Defendable receipt anchored under your name. If you lose it, you lose access to past work. The architecture is built so that nobody — not us, not Hedera, not ENS — can restore it for you. The cost of sovereignty is responsible key management.

Once registered, your HACKER is now a node on the AtlasOS network at <your-name>.atlasos.eth. Other HACKERs can resolve you. You can resolve them. You can sign documents and ship deeds.

Step 3 · Fund your wallet

Most AtlasOS services run on x402 micropayments — your wallet pays per call autonomously. To use AIOV, Rainmaker, or any third-party agent service, your wallet needs USDC.

Three paths:

If you havePathTime
USDC on Base or OptimismSend to your wallet address (shown in the HACKER UI)~30 sec
USD in a Coinbase / Kraken / Binance accountBuy USDC, withdraw to your wallet~5 min
USD in a US bank onlyUse the HACKER's onboarding flow → ACH bridge to USDC (KYC required)1–3 days first time

Your HACKER ships with a $50 USDC starter credit for your first AIOV on us. That's enough to test the flow end to end before you commit to topping up.

Step 4 · Place your first AIOV

Drop a property document (rent roll, T-12, lease, OM — any one of these is enough to start) onto the HACKER's inbox surface. From your provisioning device:

https://<your-name>.atlasos.eth/inbox

Or speak to your HACKER directly:

"Hacker, AIOV my property at 1805 Justin Road, Flower Mound, TX."

Your HACKER:

Parses your documents on-box

Granite Docling reads every page locally. Files never leave your hardware.

Encrypts the brief

Your HACKER generates a one-time session key, encrypts a small JSON brief (locked numbers · no raw PDFs).

Calls aiov.eth via x402

Atlas-70B in an ephemeral container responds with 402 Payment Required → your wallet pays $499 USDC autonomously → encrypted report returns.

Renders on-box

Your HACKER decrypts with your wallet, renders the AIOV report locally, drops it in your inbox.

Anchors the receipt

Hedera anchors the session metadata at aiov.defendable.eth/<id>. Counsel can verify the work happened — without seeing what was analyzed.

Total wall-clock time: 30 to 60 minutes. Total work for you: drop the docs, read the report.

Step 5 · Read the receipt

Every AIOV ships with a receipt URL in the report footer. It looks like:

aiov.defendable.eth/<your-deal-short-id>

Hand this URL to your CFO, your CTO, your counsel, your QI. They paste it in any browser. They see:

  • The session ID (random uuid)
  • Who requested it (your subdomain)
  • What service produced it (atlas70b.atlasos.eth)
  • When the work happened (start + end timestamps)
  • The TPM attestation of the ephemeral container
  • The Hedera transaction proving the anchor

What they can never see:

  • The deal data
  • The property address
  • The tenant name
  • The financials
  • The valuation conclusion

That's by design. The chain proves the work was done. The content stays sovereign.
